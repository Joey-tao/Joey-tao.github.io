About Me

I am Yitao Qiao, a recent graduate (June 2024) with a Master's degree in Automation from the School of Automation Science and Engineering, South China University of Technology (SCUT), where I was advised by Prof. Wenxiong Kang. I also received my Bachelor's degree in Automation from the same school in 2021.

My research interests lie in computer vision, biometrics, and multimodal learning. I am dedicated to developing more accurate and secure multimodal biometric authentication technologies. I have published several papers in top-tier journals, including IEEE TPAMI, TIFS, and IJCV.

Normalized-Full-Palmar-Hand: Towards More Accurate Hand-Based Multimodal Biometrics
Yitao Qiao*, Wenxiong Kang*, Dacan Luo, Junduan Huang.
IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (TPAMI) (CCF-A)
[PDF] [Code] [Project] [Abstract]

Abstract: (This abstract is synthesized from your CV. Please replace with the official abstract.) We proposed the "Normalized-Full-Palmar Hand" (NFPH) method, which decouples multiple hand biometric modalities, enabling comprehensive trait utilization for authentication. We designed and built a synchronous NIR and visible-light imaging device, enabling real-time dynamic hand positioning and clear multispectral imaging. We also released two open-access datasets (SCUT_NFPH_v1/v2), providing pixel-level full hand images, semantic masks, and normalized images, totaling 157,500 images. Furthermore, we introduced FPHandNet, a feature extraction and authentication network based on the NFPH method, and a lightweight Hand Segmentation and Alignment Network (HSANet) for real-time segmentation under diverse imaging conditions.

Wenwei Song, Wenxiong Kang, Adams Wai-Kin Kong, Yufeng Zhang, Yitao Qiao.
International Journal of Computer Vision (IJCV) (CCF-A)
[PDF] [Code] [Abstract]

Abstract: (This is a placeholder abstract. Please replace with the official abstract.) This paper introduces LA3M (Linear Adaptive Additive Angular Margin Loss), a novel loss function designed for video-based hand gesture authentication. This loss function adaptively adjusts the angular margin, enhancing intra-class compactness and inter-class separability, thereby achieving state-of-the-art performance in dynamic and complex hand gesture video authentication tasks.
