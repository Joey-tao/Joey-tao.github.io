About Me

I am Yitao Qiao, a recent graduate (June 2024) with a Master's degree in Automation from the School of Automation Science and Engineering, South China University of Technology (SCUT), where I was advised by Prof. Wenxiong Kang. I also received my Bachelor's degree in Automation from the same school in 2021.

My research interests lie in computer vision, biometrics, and multimodal learning. I am dedicated to developing more accurate and secure multimodal biometric authentication technologies. I have published several papers in top-tier journals, including IEEE TPAMI, TIFS, and IJCV.

Guangzhou Baiyun Airport Flight Node Guarantee System

This system provides intelligent guarantees for flight nodes at Guangzhou Baiyun Airport. Key technologies include multi-object tracking, pedestrian detection, small object recognition, and pedestrian re-identification.

Hand-based Multimodal Biometric System

A commercialized multimodal biometric system based on hand features (palm print, vein, geometry). This project represents a comprehensive application of software, hardware, algorithms, and embedded systems.

VLA (Vision-Language-Action) Grasping Project

This project uses a RealMan manipulator and a smart hand to grasp an apple and move it to a specified location, controlled by the Maniflow algorithm.
